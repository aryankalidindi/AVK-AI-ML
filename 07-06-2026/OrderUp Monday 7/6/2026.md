recap notes: Good news, and it materially changes the math in your favor. The free path is real and current:

- Gemini has computer-use built into its Flash model, and it's on the free tier via Google AI Studio — no credit card. It was designed exactly for driving a browser: see the screen, reason, click/type. (Google's announcement (https://blog.google/innovation-and-ai/models-and-research/gemini-models/introducing-computer-use-gemini-3-5-flash/), Gemini computer-use docs (https://ai.google.dev/gemini-api/docs/computer-use))
- Free-tier limits: roughly 10 requests/minute and 250 requests/day on Flash (1,000/day on Flash-Lite). (rate limits (https://ai.google.dev/gemini-api/docs/rate-limits)) Since each "look → act" step is one request and an order might take 15–40 steps, that's about 6–15 full orders per day for free — plenty for personal use. The 10/min cap means an order takes a few minutes, not seconds.

So: full auto from your phone, at $0, is genuinely viable. And a bonus — because the agent sees the screen, it can read the cart total visually, which fixes the "cart total: not read" gap we had, so your review notification gets a real number.

Here's the architecture I'd recommend, and why. We keep everything we already fought for — real Chrome, the Cloudflare bypass, the Playwright browser, the state machine, the confirm gate — and we swap only the item-building step: instead of brittle scripted selectors, an AI loop drives the browser (screenshot → Gemini picks an action → Playwright executes → repeat) toward the goal "add one McChicken from McDonald's, stop at the cart." It slots in behind the same DoorDashAutomation seam as a new CART_MODE=ai, so the semi-manual and (dead) scripted modes stay as fallbacks.

I'd build the agent loop in-house in TypeScript rather than adopting a Python framework like browser-use — three reasons: we already have Playwright + real-Chrome + Cloudflare working in TS, so we reuse all of it; it stays one codebase/one language; and most importantly, the "never click Place Order without your confirmation" gate lives in our own code, not inside a third-party agent we'd have to trust.
